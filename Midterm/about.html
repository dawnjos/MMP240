ABOUT HIPSTAR

HIPSTAR is a US magazine launched in 2018 under the Conde Nast publication, HIPSTAR targets the future generation. Like TEENVOGUE and many other magazine under Conde Nast publication it includes stories about fashion but in HIPSTAR we take it up a level and also focus on the current events of our country and the world.

The magazine has cut back on its print distribution in favor of online content, which has grown significantly. Our magazine has also expanded its focus from fashion and beauty to include politics and current affairs.

HIPSTAR magazine offers a voice to the young who have an opinion and need somewhere to voice it. That is why we feature young people who has an impact on society and striving to make changes. HIPSTAR is mostly an online entertainment and news magazine with one purpose “our future generation.”

Let's Get Personal with Yara

Michelle Obama Wrote Black-ish’s Yara Shahidi a Letter of Recommendation for College. College-application season is just about done, much to the imminent relief of a specific breed of teens with well-plotted career goals, joiner mentalities, and a hearty stomach for creative exaggeration. And, as any properly panicked applicant knows, students today need unique interests to separate them from a pack of similarly high-achieving data-entry experts. Yara Shahidi clearly read the relevant message boards, because the Black-ish actress has Michelle Obama in her back pocket. Shahidi revealed to HIPSTAR Magazine that Obama wrote her a letter of recommendation for colleges. And the mentorship is real, because the former FLOTUS went far beyond your average overworked high-school guidance counselor, also giving Shahidi a “go get ’em, tiger” back-rub as encouragement for Shahidi’s AP exams. Shahidi previously shared plans to apply to Harvard and other top schools, so with Obama’s endorsement, who’s to say? She might just have a shot.

Yara Sayeh Shahidi was born on the 10th of February 10, 2000 in Minneapolis, Minnesota. Her name ‘Yara’ is of Persian Origin and it means “someone who is capable to do something hard”. From a young age, Yara got involved in modelling and built a successful career for herself. She has since evolved into an actress with name recognition all around the world. Yara Shahidi comes from a close-knit and supportive family. Her parents, Afshin and Keri, relocated the family to California when Yara was just four years old for her father’s career as a photographer. She has two younger brothers – Sayeed and Ehsan. Sayeed followed his big sister’s footsteps and entered the entertainment industry becoming an actor and model. He is best known for his roles in Switched At Birth, The Event and The First Family. Yara Shahidi is of mixed ethnicity with her mother – Keri Salter Shahidi – being of Choctaw and African American descent while her father, Afshin Shahidi, is of Iranian descent. Yara has joined the league of well-known celebrities of mixed ethnicity who are trailblazing all over Hollywood.

It’s been three days since Zoey Johnson — the oldest child in ABC’s “black-ish” family, played by Yara Shahidi — departed for the fictional Cal U. And already, her father is a shell of a man, begging her to come home for the weekend. “Whyyyyyy???” Anthony Anderson, as the paterfamilias Dre, wails from the confines of his walk-in closet. “It hurts so bad.” “What hurts so bad, Dad?” Zoey asks with equal parts loving tolerance and eye-rolling disdain. “My soul!” he whimpers. But Zoey? She’s definitely too cool for school in “grown-ish,” a spinoff of her own having its premiere on Wednesday, Jan. 3, on Freeform. Until she’s not. We’re not. “In the very first scene, she is like every other moment: extremely confident in who she is and still kind of annoyed by her father,” Ms. Shahidi said. “But pretty immediately it’s revealed that she has no clue what she’s doing in college, and we kept Zoey amid that crisis. I think that’s what the show really is about: how one forms relationships and their personal ethics in this new time.” It’s a time that Ms. Shahidi, who turns 18 in February, is preparing for during a gap year before heading to Harvard. “I’ve been acting since kindergarten,” she said, “so I found it really important to take my time in this next step of my journey and appreciate it.”
